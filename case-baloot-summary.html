Case Study Summary

Baloot

Baloot introduced micro-saving and investment for first-time users. This case highlights trust-centered UX decisions and measurable outcomes.

Read full case study Back to work

Project snapshot

RoleUX/UI Designer
Timeline2020-2022
TeamStartup squad
DomainFintech B2C

Challenge

The main challenge was reducing fear and ambiguity in financial actions for users with low prior investment experience.

Discovery

I mapped anxiety points in onboarding and money-related actions, then prioritized language, hierarchy, and state clarity as primary levers.

Goals & opportunities

1Lower onboarding friction for new users
2Increase confidence in save/invest decisions
3Clarify state changes and feedback loops
4Build a trustworthy visual and interaction language

What I did (Responsibilities)

I designed end-to-end UX/UI for core journeys, from framing and wireframes to final interface behavior and handoff-ready artifacts.

Key decisions

1) Reduced cognitive load in first-time flows. 2) Used transparent states and outcomes. 3) Applied progressive disclosure for advanced concepts.

Final design shots

Shot 1Onboarding and trust-building screens
Shot 2Savings setup and action confirmation
Shot 3Portfolio tracking and status feedback
Shot 4Final fintech UI consistency pass

Impact

Impact 1150K downloads
Impact 2~EUR1.2M saved by users
Impact 3Higher completion in critical flows
Impact 4Stronger trust perception in product experience

Outcome & learnings

In fintech, confidence is a product metric. Clarity in wording, feedback, and sequencing directly improves adoption and retention.
