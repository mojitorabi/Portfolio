Case Study Summary

Shams-ol-Emareh

This project explored AR-assisted guidance for museum visitors. The goal was practical orientation and better narrative understanding.

Read full case study Back to work

Project snapshot

RoleUX Research + IA
TimelineProject-based
TeamMultidisciplinary
DomainAR + Culture

Challenge

Visitors needed navigation and context support without breaking immersion or creating interaction overload in a physical environment.

Discovery

I studied visitor behavior, wayfinding pain points, and museum context constraints to identify where AR adds value and where simplicity is better.

Goals & opportunities

1Improve orientation across museum flow
2Support narrative understanding at key points
3Keep interactions lightweight and contextual
4Avoid novelty that distracts from place experience

What I did (Responsibilities)

I led UX research, information architecture, and interaction design; translated insights into practical AR guidance logic.

Key decisions

1) Interaction should stay lightweight. 2) Context over novelty. 3) Orientation first, depth second.

Final design shots

Shot 1AR wayfinding entry touchpoints
Shot 2Context overlays near exhibits
Shot 3Narrative cue interactions
Shot 4Final content hierarchy states

Impact

Impact 1Pilot-ready flow in Golestan context
Impact 2Clearer visitor progression across route
Impact 3Lower confusion in orientation moments
Impact 4More meaningful cultural interaction

Outcome & learnings

In cultural experiences, technology should amplify place, not compete with it. Utility and calm interaction patterns matter most.
