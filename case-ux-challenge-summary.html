Case Study Summary

5 Day UX Challenge

A time-boxed eBay seller portal challenge focused on delivering a coherent and defensible solution under strict constraints.

Read full case study Back to work

Project snapshot

RoleUX/UI Designer
Timeline5-day sprint
TeamIndividual challenge
DomainB2B platform

Challenge

The task required simplifying complex seller workflows in limited time while preserving essential controls and information.

Discovery

I quickly framed assumptions, prioritized high-frequency tasks, and narrowed scope to protect quality and end-to-end coherence.

Goals & opportunities

1Define a realistic and focused sprint scope
2Create clear task-first flow structure
3Produce decision-ready interface direction
4Deliver artifacts that communicate rationale fast

What I did (Responsibilities)

I handled the full process: framing, workflow design, interface direction, and synthesis into presentation-ready outputs.

Key decisions

1) Scope discipline first. 2) One primary goal per screen. 3) Consistent interaction language across the flow.

Final design shots

Shot 1Problem framing and flow map
Shot 2Primary task-state wireframes
Shot 3Final concept UI direction
Shot 4Packaged sprint storytelling assets

Impact

Impact 1Fast and coherent output in 5 days
Impact 2Strong narrative from problem to solution
Impact 3High clarity for stakeholders/reviewers
Impact 4Reusable sprint decision framework

Outcome & learnings

Speed without scope discipline creates noise. Clear priorities and structure make rapid work meaningful and credible.
